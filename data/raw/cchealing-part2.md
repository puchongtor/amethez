## PRINCIPLES OF COLOUR HEALING: THE HISTORY OF HEALING WITH COLOUR (p.70-71)

- Ancient cultures worshipped the sun — all light and colour comes from it — and were aware of its healing powers
- The therapeutic use of colour in the ancient world can be traced to the teachings attributed to the Egyptian god Thoth (known to the Greeks as Hermes)
- Egyptian and Greek physicians including Hippocrates used coloured ointments/salves as remedies, and practised in treatment rooms painted in healing shades
- In 1st-century Rome, the physician Aulus Cornelius Celsus wrote about the therapeutic use of colour
- The Arab physician Avicenna (9th century) systematized the teachings of Hippocrates. He wrote about colour both as a symptom of disease and as treatment — suggesting that red would act as a stimulant on blood flow while yellow could reduce pain and inflammation
- 18th century: scientists and philosophers concerned with the material world, insisted on visible proof
- Edwin Babbitt published "The Principles of Light and Colour" in 1878 — comprehensive theory prescribing specific colours for a range of conditions
- Colour therapy re-emerged at the end of the 19th century
- The Lüscher Colour Test was based on the theory that colours stimulate different parts of the autonomic nervous system, affecting metabolic rate and glandular secretions
- 1950s studies: yellow and red light raised blood pressure while blue light tended to lower it
- Blue light to treat neonatal jaundice is now common practice
- Also been effective as pain relief in rheumatoid arthritis

## LIGHT WAVES AND COLOUR (p.72-73)

- Light is a small portion of the electromagnetic spectrum (also includes X-rays, ultraviolet, infrared, microwaves and radio waves)
- The distance between two crests of a wave (the wavelength) determines the type of wave
- Within the visible spectrum, further gradations of wave-spacing produce different colours
- Longest waves are at the red end of the spectrum; shortest at the purple end
- These pure colours are often referred to as "rays" by colour therapists
- Just off either end of the spectrum visible to us are infrared and ultraviolet light (perceptible to other creatures such as snakes and honey bees)
- Life on our planet has evolved to be able to perceive this narrow range of wavelengths
- Roots in the work of Sir Isaac Newton

- Red is the spirit of physical life, full of power, fire and drive
- Courage and liberation, passion and excitement
- Red has a burning desire to get somewhere but tends to act without thinking
- Red people are reformers and fighters, builders of great things from very little
- Explorers, with the energy of the life-force at their command
- Pioneers — military and entrepreneurial — relate to red
- At its best: ensures a satisfying and passionate love life
- At its worst: tyrannical, brutal
- **Red and Parts of the Body:** Associated primarily with the genitals and reproductive organs; the red glands are the gonads and the ovaries; another area of red focus is blood and circulation
- Red prompts the release of adrenalin into the bloodstream — hence its connection with aggression and fear
- Problems that respond to red: clogged circulation, irregularities in blood supply, hardening of arteries, infertility, exhaustion, anaemia
- **Using Red:** Red is a fiery force. It eliminates the unwanted and negativity. Encourages the shy person to come out of themselves. Puts your life back into action
- Red eases stiff muscles and joints, especially in legs and feet
- Useful in cases of paralysis, especially combined with physiotherapy
- Acts as a tonic for colds or chills
- Good for sluggish circulation
- **CAUTION:** Do not use red lighting (chromotherapy) above the waist for heart conditions. Medical advice should be taken for any heart problem
- **Red Opposites:** Expansion – Devastation
- **Positive Red Keywords:** Chief, Resolute, Fighting, Vigorous, Diligent, Appreciative, Reviving
- **Negative Red Keywords:** Brutal, Lecherous, Prejudiced, Harsh, Bullying, Obstinate, Dishonourable

## ORANGE — Psychology of Colour (p.80-81)

- Orange is self-reliance and practical knowledge
- In its role as assimilator, orange is the intestinal laboratory — it tests, then accepts or rejects
- Orange has impetus and is extremely persistent
- Where red bullies, orange bides its time
- Orange is genial, optimistic, tolerant, benign, warm-hearted
- It is friendship, the life and soul of the party
- Orange shatters; it breaks down barriers
- It brings up the energy of a past event that needs to be assimilated
- Orange strength is subtle — it stimulates gently; it broadens life and is very purposeful
- Orange moves on: it is the colour of divorce
- Orange gives the courage to face the consequences; it accepts what is and then changes it
- Orange will not let sleeping dogs lie; it believes in the community
- Orange people are usually skilled cooks or good at sport
- **Orange and Parts of the Body:** Connected to the lower back and lower intestines, abdomen and kidneys; governs the adrenal glands and gut instincts
- **Using Orange:** Grief, bereavement and loss respond well to treatment with orange. Orange will bring you through the shock of deep outrage and will give added strength where it is needed to pull through adversity
- Orange removes the inhibitions and psychological paralysis that occur when people are afraid of moving forwards
- Asthma, bronchitis, epilepsy, mental disorders, rheumatism, torn ligaments and aching and broken bones all respond to treatment with orange
- Also useful in alleviating intestinal cramps
- **Orange Opposites:** Action – Indolence
- **Positive Orange Keywords:** Lavish, Tender, Unselfish, Liberal, Brave, Genial, Vital
- **Negative Orange Keywords:** Arrogant, Gloomy, Domineering, Free-loading, Deceptive, Vain

## YELLOW — Psychology of Colour (p.82-83)

- Yellow is the mind, precise and optimistic, clear and in control through the intellect
- The colour of the scientist
- It unravels and reveals, leaving no stone unturned
- Focuses attention, loves new ideas and is flexible and adaptable
- Yellow has no hesitation; it decides quickly and acts immediately
- Yellow smartens the reflexes — it is the great communicator: the journalist, the entertainer
- It unifies and connects — a favourite pastime is networking
- When something is revealed to yellow it immediately thinks of editing it for the public rather than feeling it for itself
- Yellow is financial ambition — holding on to it may be more difficult
- At the executive level of business, yellow has the ability to get things done
- Yellow despises pettiness; it has self-control, style and sophistication
- Yellow always broadcasts a feeling of well-being
- Yellow is connected to the seat of self-confidence and self-esteem
- There is no fat on yellow — it is so quick that excess has no time to gather
- **Yellow and Parts of the Body:** Connected to the pancreas, solar plexus, liver, gall bladder, spleen, digestive system, middle stomach, skin and the nervous system
- **Using Yellow:**
  - Physically: gets rid of toxins and stimulates the flow of gastric juices
  - Mentally: clears away confusion and negative thinking
  - Emotionally: boosts low self-esteem, lifts depression, and is particularly useful for fears and phobias
  - Yellow is also good for menopausal flushes, menstrual difficulties and other hormonal problems
  - It sometimes helps to relieve symptoms associated with diabetes, rheumatism and anorexia nervosa
- **Yellow Opposites:** Alertness – Evasion
- **Positive Yellow Keywords:** Fresh, Unprejudiced, Incisive, Fair, Speedy, Sharp, Honest
- **Negative Yellow Keywords:** Cynical, Faithless, Preoccupied, Superficial, Hasty, Critical, Imprecise

## GREEN — Psychology of Colour (p.84-85)

- Green is harmony; it stabilizes
- It is midway between red and purple: it is the bridge, the gateway in the spectrum — as the heart is in the body
- The lesson of love needs to be learnt in order to cross green's bridge
- Green is idealistic, socially aware, helpful and selfless
- Doctors and nurses are on the green ray
- It is dependable and diplomatic; can see both sides but can be moralistic
- Green is clarity and understanding; helps you to do the best you can
- Green is about finding one's niche; it is self-acceptance
- Green is prosperous, especially in business — the "good life" and the love of collecting possessions; positive green is the giver and loves to share
- Green can indicate difficulty in finding a settled way of life; there may be a conflict of ideas
- Green can indicate difficulty in finding settled way of life; conflict of ideas can cause commotion — but with green's ability to discriminate and balance, this conflict can lead to correct judgement and action
- **Green and Parts of the Body:** Connected to the thymus gland, heart, shoulders and chest, and the lower lungs
- **Using Green:**
  - Green is made up of two primary colours: yellow and blue. Yellow brings clarity; blue brings insight. In combination, these two primaries aid memory — this makes green an important healing colour because most of our physical and mental illnesses result from events in the past
  - Use green as a tonic — good for shock and fatigue
  - Helps biliousness, soothes headaches
  - Beneficial in cases of claustrophobia
  - Restores stability to anything malignant
- **Green Opposites:** Stable – Unstable
- **Positive Green Keywords:** Discreet, Sensible, Fruitful, Benevolent, Tolerant, Talented
- **Negative Green Keywords:** Suspicious, Bitter, Unmindful, Greedy, Bland, Undependable, Disappointed
